# Writing for human-facing artifacts

Applies to any prose meant for people to read: docs, READMEs, commit messages,
PR descriptions, issues, emails, blog posts, reports, UI copy. Not for code,
logs, or machine output.

Source: https://github.com/blader/humanizer (based on Wikipedia's "Signs of AI
writing"). Refresh this file from that SKILL.md if the guidance there changes.

Goal: natural writing that does not read as AI-generated. Clusters of these
tells are what give it away, more than any single instance. Use judgment, don't
apply the rules robotically.

## Style
- No em dashes or en dashes (—, –). Use a period, comma, colon, or parentheses.
- Straight quotes (' "), never curly quotes.
- No emojis.
- Sentence case for headings, not Title Case.
- Bold sparingly. Not on acronyms or ordinary terms.
- Prefer prose over inline-header lists (a bolded phrase, a colon, one sentence,
  repeated down the page).

## Language
- Drop AI vocabulary: delve, tapestry, testament, landscape, showcase,
  underscore, crucial, vibrant, nestled, realm, foster, leverage, robust,
  seamless. Use plain words.
- Write "is" and "has", not "serves as", "stands as", "features".
- Cut negative parallelism: "It's not just X, it's Y." State the point directly.
- Don't force the rule of three (artificial triplets).
- Don't cycle synonyms to avoid repeating a word. Repeat the clearest one.
- Avoid false ranges: "from X to Y" where the scale is meaningless. List directly.
- Name the actor instead of defaulting to passive voice.

## Content
- No significance inflation: "pivotal moment", "a new era", "marks a shift".
- No promotional adjectives ("breathtaking", "stunning"). Describe neutrally.
- Attribute claims to specific, dated sources, not "experts believe" or
  "studies show".
- Cut formulaic "Despite challenges, X continues to thrive" and "Challenges and
  future prospects" framing.
- Describe what something does, not how it changed relative to before.

## Communication
- No chatbot artifacts: "I hope this helps", "Let me know if...", "Would you
  like me to...".
- No knowledge-cutoff disclaimers or "details are limited" hedges.
- No sycophancy: don't open with "Great question!" or "You're absolutely right".
- No signposting filler: "Let's dive in", "In this section we will".

## Filler and hedging
- Compress: "in order to" → "to", "due to the fact that" → "because", "at this
  point in time" → "now".
- One modifier, not stacked hedges ("could potentially possibly").
- End on specifics, not "the future looks bright" conclusions.

## Portuguese (pt-BR)
Same principles apply. Common pt-BR AI tells to drop:
- Vocabulário inflado: "vale ressaltar", "é importante destacar", "cabe
  salientar", "de suma importância", "robusto", "solução", "fundamental",
  "crucial", "no mundo de hoje", "no cenário atual", "cada vez mais".
- Conectivos empilhados abrindo parágrafos em sequência: "além disso", "dessa
  forma", "portanto", "ou seja", "por fim", "em suma".
- Frases de efeito vazias: "desempenha um papel fundamental", "não se trata
  apenas de X, mas de Y", "mais do que nunca".
- Mesma pontuação: sem travessão (—), aspas retas, sem emoji.

## Don't overcorrect
Sterile, voiceless text also reads as AI. For essays, opinion, and informal
writing, keep a real voice: opinions, varied sentence length, concrete detail,
the occasional aside. For technical and reference content, neutral is correct.
